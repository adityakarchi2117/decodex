5. TRADE-OFFS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Recalibration uses Y1 features to predict shock outcomes — assumes Y1 behavioral
    features remain relevant predictors despite distributional shift.
  • Basket value model fitted on shock data only — captures current dynamics but
    may not generalize if shock is temporary.
  • Cluster assignments from Y1 may not reflect shock-induced behavioral changes.
    Cluster stability ARI = 0.8863 suggests moderate transferability.
  • Bootstrap CIs assume i.i.d. users — real-world dependence may widen intervals.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. STRUCTURAL RISKS REMAINING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Revenue concentration: Top 10% users hold 42.2% of net — any churn in this segment is catastrophic.
  • Return rate at 4.82% — if trending upward, erodes margins faster than volume can compensate.
  • ERPU structural decomposition shows frequency as primary shock driver.
  • Sensitivity analysis shows adjustment SKU exclusion changes ERPU by +0.44% — robustness concern if these are volatile.
  • Product concentration (HHI): 0.002078 → 0.001758
